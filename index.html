Hello!

My name is Martin Sielfeld, and I'm a predoc research fellow at Yale University's Tobin Center for Economic Policy. My research experience revolves around the Economics of Education, specifically in information asymmetries, digitalization, and competition. I'm also interested in Industrial Organization and urban economics.

I got my Master's in Economics from Universidad Adolfo Ibáñez in 2021. Since then, I have participated in research projects in many parts of Latin America, the Caribbean, Europe, and the US. This exposure to a broad set of educational settings allowed me to gather knowledge about common problems. At the same time, this experience also taught me how algorithms and new technology could solve or attenuate specific issues, such as information friction, resource allocation, and financial efficiency.
